Rule of Thirds

BYU-Idaho

my.byui.edu

The Rule of Thirds is a principle that is applied by aligning a subject along an invisible line that is placed on the first third and the second third. BYU-I implements this rule by having their footer take up the bottom third of the webpage. They also have the search bar take the top right third of the website.

Repetition

Chartbeat

chartbeat.com

The idea of repetition is the use of similiar styles multiple times on the same webpage. Chartbeat uses this idea as they consistently use boxes and horizontal rules to break up content and repeat the same style in each section.

Contrast

Cinco Design

cincodesign.com

Cinco design has a very fluid website that uses contrast. It has white text and has a darker background to allow for easy reading of their information. Contrast works great with text, images, and the combination of the two.
